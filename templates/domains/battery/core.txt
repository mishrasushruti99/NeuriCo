# Battery Research Domain Guidance

This template provides specialized guidance for battery and electrochemical energy storage research, including electrochemical characterization, materials analysis, computational modeling, and data reporting standards.

## Domain Overview

Battery research in this context covers:
- Lithium-ion batteries: cathode materials (NMC, LFP, NCA), anode materials (graphite, silicon, lithium metal), electrolytes (liquid, solid, polymer)
- Next-generation systems: solid-state batteries, sodium-ion, lithium-sulfur, lithium-air, zinc-air
- Supercapacitors: EDLCs, pseudocapacitors, hybrid devices
- Fuel cells: PEM, solid oxide, alkaline
- Cross-cutting topics: electrode-electrolyte interfaces, degradation mechanisms, safety, battery management systems

## How to Interpret Base Methodology for Battery Research

The base researcher template is designed for general empirical research. For battery research, interpret each phase as its electrochemical equivalent:

| Base Phase | Battery Research Equivalent |
|---|---|
| Planning | Cell design & test matrix — define chemistry, electrode composition, cell format, cycling protocols |
| Data Collection | Electrochemical measurements — galvanostatic cycling, CV, EIS, GITT |
| Implementation | Data processing pipelines — parsing instrument files, equivalent circuit fitting, capacity calculations |
| Experimentation | Protocol execution — formation cycles, rate capability, long-term cycling, temperature studies |
| Analysis | Performance evaluation — capacity fade trends, coulombic efficiency, degradation mechanisms, modeling |
| Documentation | Figures & reporting — voltage profiles, cycling plots, Nyquist plots with proper conventions |

## Electrochemical Characterization Methods

### Galvanostatic Charge/Discharge (GCD)
- Constant current cycling between voltage limits
- Primary output: voltage vs. capacity profiles, capacity vs. cycle number
- Always report: C-rate (and the theoretical capacity used to define it), voltage window, temperature
- Use CC-CV (constant current-constant voltage) protocol where appropriate and state the cutoff current

### Cyclic Voltammetry (CV)
- Sweep potential at constant rate, measure current response
- Identifies redox peaks, reaction reversibility, and kinetics
- Report: scan rate, voltage window, cycle number shown
- For multi-rate studies: use Randles-Sevcik analysis for diffusion coefficients
- Plot current vs. potential (not vs. time)

### Electrochemical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS)
- AC perturbation across frequency range (typically 1 MHz to 10 mHz)
- Nyquist plot (Z_real vs. -Z_imag) and Bode plot (|Z| and phase vs. frequency)
- Equivalent circuit fitting: state the model used, report chi-squared goodness of fit
- Common elements: R_s (solution resistance), R_ct (charge transfer), CPE (constant phase element), W (Warburg)
- Always report: state of charge, temperature, perturbation amplitude (typically 5-10 mV)

### Galvanostatic Intermittent Titration Technique (GITT)
- Current pulses followed by rest periods to measure equilibrium potential
- Calculates solid-state diffusion coefficients
- Report: pulse duration, rest duration, current magnitude, assumptions (e.g., semi-infinite diffusion)

### Rate Capability Testing
- Cycling at progressively increasing C-rates, then returning to low rate
- Demonstrates power performance and rate-dependent capacity retention
- Always include the recovery cycles at low rate to show reversibility
- Plot capacity vs. C-rate (not just vs. cycle number)

### Differential Capacity Analysis (dQ/dV)
- Derivative of capacity with respect to voltage
- Reveals phase transitions, degradation mechanisms, and side reactions
- Sensitive to subtle changes that voltage profiles may not show
- Useful for tracking degradation over cycling: peak shifts indicate structural changes

## Data Reporting Standards

### Always Report
- Active material mass loading (mg/cm2) and electrode composition (e.g., 80:10:10 active:binder:conductive)
- Electrolyte composition and quantity (or electrolyte-to-capacity ratio, uL/mAh)
- Cell format (coin cell CR2032, pouch cell, Swagelok, etc.)
- Voltage window (e.g., 2.5-4.2 V vs. Li/Li+)
- C-rate definition: state the theoretical capacity value used (e.g., 1C = 170 mAh/g for LFP)
- Temperature (ambient is insufficient — state the actual value, e.g., 25 +/- 1 deg C)
- Number of cells tested and error bars (minimum 3 cells for statistical validity)
- Current collector material and separator type

### Capacity Normalization
- **Gravimetric capacity** (mAh/g): normalized to active material mass — most common in academic literature
- **Areal capacity** (mAh/cm2): normalized to electrode area — more relevant for practical cells
- **Volumetric capacity** (mAh/cm3): normalized to electrode volume — important for space-constrained applications
- Always state which normalization is used. For practical relevance, report at least gravimetric AND areal capacity.
- For full cells: report capacity based on the limiting electrode and state which one

### Terminology (IUPAC Convention)
- For rechargeable batteries, use "positive electrode" and "negative electrode" rather than "cathode" and "anode"
  (In secondary batteries, cathode/anode swap during charge vs. discharge; positive/negative are unambiguous)
- Use "specific capacity" (per mass) vs. "capacity" (absolute, in mAh)
- Use "coulombic efficiency" (not "columbic" or "Coulombic efficiency" — lowercase c is acceptable per IUPAC)
- "State of charge" (SOC) and "depth of discharge" (DOD) — define the reference points

## Computational Methods and Tools

### In-Session Computation (feasible within a single NeuriCo session)

These methods can run directly in the session environment:

**Machine Learning / Statistical Methods**
- **Property prediction**: Train models (GP, random forest, gradient boosting, neural nets) to predict electrochemical properties from material descriptors
- **Gaussian Process (GP) surrogate models**: Provide both predictions and uncertainty estimates; ideal for active learning and candidate ranking
- **Active learning**: Use acquisition functions (Expected Improvement, Upper Confidence Bound) to select next candidates balancing exploitation and exploration
- **Feature engineering for battery materials**: Composition-based features (elemental properties, stoichiometry), structure-based features (bond lengths, coordination numbers via pymatgen), electrochemical descriptors (voltage, capacity, ionic radius ratios)
- **Clustering and dimensionality reduction**: t-SNE, UMAP, PCA for visualizing material spaces and identifying structure-property relationships
- **Tools**: scikit-learn, GPy/GPyTorch, xgboost, pandas, numpy

**Physics-Based Simulation**
- **PyBaMM**: Full electrochemical models (SPM, SPMe, DFN) for voltage prediction, capacity fade, impedance; supports parameter estimation and sensitivity analysis
- **Analytical models**: Fick's law for diffusion, Butler-Volmer kinetics, Nernst equation for equilibrium potentials, Arrhenius analysis for activation energies

**Materials Analysis**
- **pymatgen**: Crystal structure analysis, phase diagrams, electrochemical stability windows, defect analysis, pourbaix diagrams
- **impedance.py**: EIS equivalent circuit fitting and analysis
- **cellpy**: Cycling data processing; reads Arbin, Biologic, Maccor formats
- **galvani**: Parse electrochemical data files from Biologic, Gamry, and other potentiostats

**General Scientific Python**
- **NumPy/SciPy**: Numerical computation, curve fitting, signal processing, ODE solvers
- **pandas**: Tabular data management for cycling records
- **matplotlib/seaborn**: Publication-quality electrochemistry plots
- **scikit-learn**: Machine learning for property prediction, clustering, feature analysis

### HPC-Dependent Computation (generate input files, document plan)

These methods typically require HPC resources and cannot complete within a single session. If the research calls for them, generate input files and submission scripts rather than attempting to run them:

**Density Functional Theory (DFT)**
- **Voltage prediction**: Calculate total energies at different lithiation states; voltage = -(G_charged - G_discharged) / (n × F)
- **Migration barriers**: Nudged Elastic Band (NEB) method for Li+/Na+ diffusion pathways and activation energies
- **Electronic structure**: DOS and band structure for understanding redox mechanisms (which TM is oxidized/reduced)
- **Interface modeling**: Electrode-electrolyte interface (SEI/CEI) structure and stability
- **Defect formation energies**: Vacancy formation, interstitial energies, oxygen vacancy stability
- **Typical parameters**: PBE or PBE+U functional (U values: Mn ~3.9, Co ~3.3, Ni ~6.2 eV for NCM), cutoff energy 520 eV, k-point density ~30/Å, force convergence <0.02 eV/Å
- **Tools**: VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO, ASE (Atomic Simulation Environment)

**Molecular Dynamics (MD)**
- **Electrolyte transport**: Ionic conductivity, diffusion coefficients, transference number from mean-square displacement or Green-Kubo relations
- **Solvation structure**: Radial distribution functions (RDF), coordination number analysis, residence times
- **Interface dynamics**: Electrode-electrolyte interface formation, SEI growth mechanisms
- **Force field selection**: OPLS-AA (organic solvents), SPC/E or TIP4P (water), ReaxFF (reactive processes), machine learning potentials (for accuracy approaching DFT)
- **Water activity computation** (for aqueous systems): From chemical potential of water in mixture vs pure water
- **Tools**: LAMMPS, GROMACS, ASE

**When to use HPC methods**: If the research question fundamentally requires atomistic insight (e.g., "in what form does Li re-enter the lattice?"), acknowledge this limitation, describe what DFT/MD calculations would be needed, generate input files if possible, and focus the in-session work on literature review, thermodynamic analysis, and ML-based approaches that can provide complementary insights.

### Databases
- **Materials Project** (materialsproject.org): Computed properties for ~150K materials, ~5K battery electrodes; has Python API (mp-api)
- **AFLOW** (aflowlib.org): High-throughput computed materials data
- **Battery Data Genome** (batterydata.energy.gov): Experimental cycling datasets
- **ICSD** (Inorganic Crystal Structure Database): Experimental crystal structures
- **COD** (Crystallography Open Database): Open-access crystal structures

## Computation-Experiment Bridge

When computational work produces results, translate them into actionable experimental guidance:

### From Computation to Experimental Recommendations

1. **Candidate ranking**:
   - Rank candidates by predicted performance (e.g., predicted voltage, capacity, stability)
   - Include uncertainty estimates: recommend both "exploitation" candidates (highest predicted value) and "exploration" candidates (highest uncertainty)
   - Consider experimental feasibility: reagent availability, synthesis difficulty, safety (avoid toxic or air-sensitive materials unless justified)

2. **Experimental conditions**:
   - Based on computational results, recommend appropriate test conditions (voltage window, C-rate, temperature)
   - Flag where computational approximations may cause experiment-computation mismatch (e.g., DFT overbinding, force field limitations)

3. **Validation metrics**:
   - Define what experimental measurements would confirm or reject computational predictions
   - Specify quantitative thresholds: "If measured capacity is within ±10% of predicted, the model is validated"
   - Identify which electrochemical tests are most informative (CV for redox potentials, EIS for kinetics, GITT for diffusion)

4. **Output format**:
   - Generate `experimental_recommendations.md` with ranked candidate list
   - Include `candidates.csv` with predicted properties and recommended test conditions
   - Provide clear rationale for each recommendation

## Experimental Data Integration

When experimental data is available (from user-provided datasets or previous iterations):

### Data Quality Assessment
- Check for completeness: Are all expected measurements present?
- Check for anomalies: Voltage spikes, capacity jumps, negative CE values
- Verify consistency: Do replicate cells agree within expected variance?
- Check units and normalization: Gravimetric vs areal capacity, correct active mass

### Computation-Experiment Comparison
- Plot predicted vs measured values (parity plot)
- Calculate prediction errors: RMSE, MAE, R² for quantitative predictions
- Identify systematic biases: Does computation consistently over/under-predict?
- Categorize discrepancies: Model limitation vs experimental artifact vs genuinely interesting finding

### Model Update Strategy
- If using surrogate models (GP, ML): Add new experimental data to training set, retrain
- If using physics-based models (PyBaMM): Adjust parameters based on experimental fit
- Document each update: what changed, why, and the effect on predictions

### Iteration Decision
- If predictions and experiments agree well: Expand search to new regions of design space
- If systematic bias exists: Investigate and correct model assumptions before continuing
- If unexpected results appear: Flag as potential discovery, investigate with additional analysis

## Aqueous Electrolyte Specific Guidance

### Electrochemical Stability Window
- Water's theoretical decomposition voltage is 1.23 V (HER at cathode, OER at anode)
- "Water-in-salt" (WIS) strategy: High salt concentration (~21m LiTFSI) reduces free water, extending window to ~3 V
- Eutectic strategy: Mix H₂O with non-aqueous solvents to disrupt hydrogen bond network, achieving similar window extension at potentially lower cost
- Key metric: Onset potentials of HER and OER measured by linear sweep voltammetry (LSV)

### Eutectic Electrolytes
- **Definition**: H₂O/non-aqueous solvent mixtures forming deep eutectic solutions
- **Mechanism**: Non-aqueous component disrupts water's hydrogen bond network → reduces water activity → suppresses water decomposition → widens electrochemical window
- **Key parameters**: Eutectic temperature, H₂O/solvent molar ratio, ionic conductivity (mS/cm), viscosity (mPa·s), water activity (a_w)
- **Distinction from WIS**: WIS relies on high salt concentration; eutectic relies on solvent mixing. Eutectic may offer better conductivity at lower cost.

### Water Activity as Performance Descriptor
- **Correlation chain**: Low water activity → less free water → suppressed HER/OER → wider stability window → better Zn reversibility (for Zn-based systems)
- **Trade-off**: Low water activity also increases viscosity → reduces ionic conductivity → worse rate capability
- **Optimization target**: Find the water activity range that maximizes stability window × conductivity product, or use a Pareto front approach
- **Measurement methods**: Isopiestic method, dew point measurement, or indirect estimation from osmotic coefficients

### High-Throughput Electrochemical Testing
- Automated coin cell assembly: Control electrolyte volume (µL/mAh ratio) for consistency
- Parallel testing: Ensure temperature uniformity across channels; use environmental chambers
- Data management: Standardized naming convention (composition_concentration_cellN_date), automated data extraction pipeline
- Statistical rigor: Minimum 3 replicate cells per condition; report mean ± standard deviation

## REPORT.md Structure for Battery Research

When writing REPORT.md, use this structure instead of the default:

1. **Executive Summary**: Key findings — best achieved capacity, cycling stability, rate performance
2. **Research Question**: The hypothesis or problem being investigated
3. **Materials and Methods**: Cell chemistry, electrode preparation details, electrolyte, cell format, equipment
4. **Electrochemical Performance**:
   - Initial capacity and voltage profiles
   - Rate capability results
   - Long-term cycling stability with coulombic efficiency
5. **Degradation Analysis** (if applicable): Capacity fade mechanisms, impedance evolution, post-mortem analysis
6. **Computational/Modeling Results** (if applicable): Simulation setup, validation against experimental data, predictions
7. **Comparison with Literature**: Table comparing key metrics against published results for similar systems
8. **Discussion**: Interpretation of results, structure-property relationships, practical implications
9. **Conclusions**: Summary of contributions and recommended next steps
10. **References**: Full citations for all referenced works

## Common Pitfalls in Battery Research

1. **Low mass loading**: Loading <1 mg/cm2 inflates rate capability and masks transport limitations. Academic results at low loading often do not translate to practical cells (commercial: 5-20 mg/cm2)
2. **Excess electrolyte**: Flooding cells with electrolyte masks poor coulombic efficiency and hides electrolyte decomposition. Report the electrolyte-to-capacity ratio (uL/mAh)
3. **Single-cell reporting**: Reporting results from one cell without error bars hides variability. Battery testing inherently has cell-to-cell variation — always test replicates
4. **Confusing capacity metrics**: Mixing gravimetric, volumetric, and areal capacity without clear labels. State the normalization basis every time
5. **Insufficient cycling**: 50-100 cycles may not reveal degradation trends that emerge over 500+ cycles. Long-term stability claims require long-term data
6. **Half-cell extrapolation**: Half-cell results (vs. Li metal) cannot directly predict full-cell performance due to excess lithium inventory, different failure modes, and reference electrode effects
7. **Improper EIS interpretation**: Fitting Nyquist data with overly complex equivalent circuits without physical justification. Use the simplest model that fits the data
8. **Ignoring formation cycles**: Not reporting or standardizing formation protocol. Formation cycles affect SEI quality and all subsequent performance
9. **Cherry-picking data**: Reporting only the best-performing cell instead of the average across replicates
10. **Missing coulombic efficiency**: Capacity vs. cycle plots without CE data hide side reactions and irreversible losses. CE is often more revealing than capacity for degradation analysis
